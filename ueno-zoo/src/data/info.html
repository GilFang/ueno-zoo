Galapagos Tortoise

Scientific Name: Chelonoidis niger

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Grasslands and desert

Diet: Herbivore

Fun Fact: They are the largest tortoises in the world and can live over 100 years.

Saltwater Crocodile

Scientific Name: Crocodylus porosus

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Coastal rivers and swamps

Diet: Carnivore

Fun Fact: They have the strongest bite of any living animal—up to 3,700 PSI.

Green Iguana

Scientific Name: Iguana iguana

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Tropical forests

Diet: Herbivore

Fun Fact: They can detach and regrow their tails if caught.

Red-Footed Tortoise

Scientific Name: Chelonoidis carbonaria

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Dry and wet forests, grasslands, savannas

Diet: Primarily herbivore, occasionally small animals

Fun Fact: Males flip rivals onto their backs during mating season fights.

Pig-Nosed Turtle

Scientific Name: Carettochelys insculpta

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Warm, tropical bodies of water

Diet: Omnivore (mostly plants)

Fun Fact: Its leathery shell makes it extra flexible for swimming.

Asian Arowana

Scientific Name: Scleropages formosus

Type: Fish

Habitat: Lowland swamps and rivers

Diet: Carnivore

Fun Fact: Also called the 'dragonfish' for its red color and jumping ability.

African Bullfrog

Scientific Name: Pyxicephalus adspersus

Type: Amphibian

Habitat: Dry and moist savanna, shrubland

Diet: Invertebrates, lizards, birds, small mammals

Fun Fact: They stay underground up to 10 months during the dry season.

Dyeing Poison Dart Frog

Scientific Name: Dendrobates tinctorius

Type: Amphibian

Habitat: Tropical forests

Diet: Insectivore

Fun Fact: Their bright colors warn predators that they’re toxic.

False Gharial

Scientific Name: Tomistoma schlegelii

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Freshwater with dense vegetation

Diet: Carnivore

Fun Fact: Their long, narrow snouts help catch fish efficiently.

Western Hognose Snake

Scientific Name: Heterodon nasicus

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Flat prairie areas with sandy soil

Diet: Toads, lizards, reptile eggs

Fun Fact: They use their upturned snouts to burrow for food.

Flamingo

Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus

Type: Bird

Habitat: Lakes, lagoons, wetlands

Diet: Algae, brine shrimp

Fun Fact: Their pink color comes from pigments in the algae and shrimp they eat.

Shoebill

Scientific Name: Balaeniceps rex

Type: Bird

Habitat: Swamps and wetlands in Central Africa

Diet: Fish, frogs, reptiles

Fun Fact: Shoebills can stand completely still for hours and catch lungfish with their huge beaks.

Giraffe

Scientific Name: Giraffa

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Savannas and open woodlands

Diet: Leaves, especially acacia

Fun Fact: They use their 45 cm long tongues to reach leaves high in the trees.

Black Rhino

Scientific Name: Diceros bicornis

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Savannas, grasslands, forests

Diet: Leaves, twigs, shrubs

Fun Fact: Their two horns grow throughout their lives, like fingernails.

Penguin

Scientific Name: Spheniscidae

Type: Bird

Habitat: Antarctic and sub-Antarctic regions, coastal areas

Diet: Fish, squid, krill

Fun Fact: Penguins can't fly, but they are excellent swimmers and slide on ice.

Ring-Tailed Lemur

Scientific Name: Lemur catta

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Forests and scrublands of Madagascar

Diet: Fruits, leaves, flowers

Fun Fact: They use scent glands to mark territory and have many vocal calls.

Hippo

Scientific Name: Hippopotamus amphibius

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Rivers, lakes, swamps

Diet: Grasses, aquatic plants

Fun Fact: Hippos stay in water during the day to keep cool and graze at night.

Asian Elephant

Scientific Name: Elephas maximus

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands

Diet: Grasses, fruits, bark, roots

Fun Fact: Asian elephants live in matriarchal groups and feed most of the day.

Sumatran Tiger

Scientific Name: Panthera tigris sumatrae

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Tropical rainforests of Sumatra

Diet: Deer, wild boar, birds

Fun Fact: They are the smallest tiger species and enjoy swimming to cool off.

Gorilla

Scientific Name: Gorilla

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Forests of Central Africa

Diet: Fruits, leaves, shoots

Fun Fact: Gorillas live in family groups and share 98.3% of our DNA.

Polar Bear

Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Arctic sea ice

Diet: Seals, fish

Fun Fact: Polar bears have black skin and translucent fur that looks white because it reflects light. They can swim for days nonstop.

Colobus Monkey

Scientific Name: Colobus

Type: Mammal (primate)

Habitat: Forests of Central and East Africa

Diet: Leaves, fruits, flowers

Fun Fact: Colobus monkeys can digest toxic plants and spend most of their time in the trees.

Black-handed Spider Monkey

Scientific Name: Ateles geoffroyi

Type: Mammal (primate)

Habitat: Tropical forests of Central America

Diet: Fruits, leaves, flowers

Fun Fact: They have prehensile tails with unique prints, used like a fifth limb for grabbing and hanging.

Japanese Macaque

Scientific Name: Macaca fuscata

Type: Mammal (primate)

Habitat: Mountains and forests of Japan (Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu)

Diet: Fruits, leaves, seeds, insects

Fun Fact: Japanese macaques, or snow monkeys, bathe in hot springs to stay warm during winter.

African White-backed Vulture

Scientific Name: Gyps africanus

Type: Bird (raptor)

Habitat: Savannas and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa

Diet: Carrion

Fun Fact: These vultures often feed in large groups and can strip a carcass in under three minutes.

Andean Condor

Scientific Name: Vultur gryphus

Type: Bird (raptor)

Habitat: Mountains and coastal regions of South America

Diet: Carrion

Fun Fact: Andean condors can soar up to 18,000 feet and are the largest flying birds in South America.

Harris Hawk

Scientific Name: Parabuteo unicinctus

Type: Bird (raptor)

Habitat: Semiarid deserts of the Americas

Diet: Small mammals, birds, reptiles

Fun Fact: Harris hawks perform 'back-standing' and are unusually social for raptors.

Steller’s Sea Eagle

Scientific Name: Haliaeetus pelagicus

Type: Bird (raptor)

Habitat: Coasts and islands of eastern Russia and northern Japan

Diet: Fish, water birds

Fun Fact: One of the rarest raptors, they breed only in eastern Russia but migrate to Hokkaido in winter.

Prairie Dog

Scientific Name: Cynomys

Type: Mammal (rodent)

Habitat: Grasslands of North America

Diet: Grasses, seeds, roots

Fun Fact: Prairie dogs have a complex communication system and a mating season lasting only one hour.

American Bison

Scientific Name: Bison bison

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Plains and prairies of North America

Diet: Grasses, herbs

Fun Fact: Bison can run faster than a horse, and calves are called 'red dogs' due to their orange-red fur.
